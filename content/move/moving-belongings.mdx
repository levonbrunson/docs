# Moving Your Personal Belongings

Repatriates can often bring belongings tax-free. Current laws align with EAEU regulations.

## Eligibility for Tax-Free Import

You may be eligible if you are:
1.  A citizen of Armenia.
2.  A permanent resident (Special or Permanent status).
3.  A refugee.

**Residency Requirement**: You must not have lived in Armenia for more than **915 days** (2.5 years) in the last **5 years** (or spent at least 9/12 days per year outside of Armenia).
*   **Registration Deadline**: You must register your address (*grantsoum*) within **180 days** of your actual arrival in Armenia.
*   **Import Timeframe**: You have **18 months** from the date of "arrival for permanent residence" (the day you received your repatriate resident's ID card or citizenship certificate) to import goods tax-free.
*   **Filing Statement**: If you had already been registered in Armenia, you must file a statement with the customs office within **3 months from the time of entry** into Armenia.

**Alternative Option**: If you haven't lived in Armenia for >12 months in the last 420 days, you can import up to **5,000 EUR** value (excluding cars).
*   *Note*: You must submit a written request within **60 days** of arrival. This option is available once every 3 years.

## What You Can Bring
*   **Personal Items**: Unlimited clothing, books, bedding, home accessories, etc.
*   **Electronics**: Max 3 of each type for personal and daily purposes (must be used and show wear/tear). If new or in original boxes, they must be cleared through customs.
*   **Furniture**: 1 complete set of each type of furniture for personal use only.
*   **Car**: 1 vehicle for personal use that you can prove you've owned for at least 6 months prior to moving.

### Packing Rules & Restrictions
*   **Condition**: Items must be **used** (no original sealed packaging or factory labeling).
*   **Weight**: Boxes should not exceed **25 kg** each.
*   **Prohibited**: Do not pack liquids, food, medication, or flammable items.
*   **Do NOT bring**: Paper products, toilet paper, and similar consumables that are easily available in Armenia.
*   **Pro Tip**: Remove all batteries from toys and electronics.
*   **Business Use**: Do not bring items for business use, construction materials (pipes, toilets), or large quantities of electronics.

## The Process

1.  **Get Status**: Obtain residency/citizenship and register your address (*grantsoum*).
    *   *Cost*: Grantsoum costs ~1,000 AMD.
    *   *Tip*: Ensure your local contact number is on your boxes so you can be notified of arrival.
2.  **Apply for Certificate**: Apply at the Passport and Visa Department for a document confirming your move for permanent residence.
    *   *Timeline*: Usually issued within **5 working days**.
3.  **Submit to Customs**: The repatriate must file an application for tax-free import with the customs office, specifying the item description and number of items imported.
    *   Send your documents and list of goods to the State Revenue Committee (*secretariat@petekamutner.am*).
    *   *Processing*: The customs office has up to **10 days** to grant or reject the application.
4.  **Clear Customs**: Go to the cargo office to pick up items.
    *   *Warehouse Fees*: **Free for the first 48 hours**. After that, fees are ~10,000 - 20,000 AMD per day depending on volume.

### Required Paperwork
*   **Proof of Status**: Document confirming RA citizenship, residency, or refugee status.
*   **Proof of Move**: Document from Passport & Visa Dept confirming move for permanent residence.
*   **Proof of Foreign Residence**: Foreign passport or residence permit confirming residence abroad.
*   **Transport Documents**: Original Bill of Lading (OBL), Packing list, Pro-forma invoice, Transportation cost info.
*   **Inventory**: Detailed inventory including values.
*   **Vehicle Documents**: Registration and ownership certificate (if importing a car).
*   **Employment Letter**: (Often requested).

*Note: Armenian translations of documents may be requested.*

### Costs & Fees
*   **Customs Clearance Fee**: ~7,000 AMD.
*   **Warehouse Fees**: Free for 48h, then daily fees apply.
*   **Other Costs**: Packing, shipping, and warehousing costs are the responsibility of the repatriate.

### EAEU Regulations
For movement of goods within the **EAEU**, customs clearance generally doesn't apply. However, since Armenia doesn't share borders with other EAEU members, if transporting by road (via Georgia), a **customs declaration** should be filled out in the exporting country.

## Shipping by Post
*   **Tax-Free Limit**: 31 kg and 200 EUR value (from non-EAEU countries).
*   **Over Limit**: 15% duty on the excess value (min 2 EUR per extra kg).

## Pets
Importing pets requires:
*   **Veterinary Health Certificate**: Clearing the animal of Rabies and other diseases.
*   **Vaccination Certificate**.
*   **Pet Passport**.
*   **Photo of the pet**.
*   **Permission from Ministry of Environment**: Required for wild animals.
