# Education in Armenia

Education is highly valued in Armenia, with a 99.8% literacy rate.

## School System
*   **Compulsory**: Ages 6-16 (through high school).
*   **Structure**:
    *   **Elementary**: Grades 1-4.
    *   **Middle**: Grades 5-9 (Basic Education Certificate).
    *   **High School**: Grades 10-12 (Full Secondary Education Certificate).
*   **Grading**: 10-point or 20-point scale (often converted).

## Enrollment
*   **Online**: Register via [hayt.emis.am](https://hayt.emis.am) (registration usually opens in June).
*   **Documents**:
    *   Parent's passport/ID.
    *   Child's birth certificate.
    *   Health certificate (Form 26).
    *   Photos.
    *   Academic records (if transferring).
*   **SSN**: Required for registration.

## Types of Schools
*   **Public**: Free. Books may have a small rental fee. Food is generally not provided (except kindergarten).
*   **Private**: Tuition fees apply. Often include food, transport, and extracurriculars.
*   **International**: *UWC Dilijan*, *QSI*, *CIS*, *Ayren*, *French School*, etc.

## Higher Education
*   **Universities**: State (YSU, Polytechnic) and International (AUA, French University, Russian-Armenian University).
*   **Vocational**: Colleges offering specialized training (3-4 years).

## Non-Formal Education
Armenia is a hub for creative tech education.
*   **TUMO**: Free creative technologies center for teens (12-18). Locations in Yerevan, Dilijan, Gyumri, etc.
*   **COAF**: SMART Centers in rural areas.
*   **Armath**: Engineering labs in schools across the country.
*   **Technology Centers**: *Gyumri Technology Center (GTC)*, *Vanadzor Technology Center (VTC)*.

## Homeschooling
Not generally an option for citizens unless there are special educational needs. International curriculums may be an exception for foreigners.
