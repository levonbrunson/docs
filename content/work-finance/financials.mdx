# Financials

Managing your finances is a crucial part of settling in.

## Moving Money to Armenia
Moving your financial assets to Armenia is relatively straightforward:

1.  **Cash**: You can bring up to **$10,000** (or equivalent) without declaration. Amounts over $10,000 must be declared at customs.
    *   *Note: Import and export of local currency is prohibited.*
2.  **Bank Transfer**: For amounts over $10,000, International SWIFT transfers are recommended.
3.  **Money Transfer Services**: Fast transfers via MoneyGram, Unistream, Best, Moneytun (USA-Armenia-USA), Intelexpress, Converse Transfer (Russia-Armenia-Russia), and RIA.
4.  **Online**: Some banks allow opening accounts online.

## Banks in Armenia
There are 18 banks operating in Armenia, including local and international subsidiaries.

### International Subsidiaries
*   **Byblos Bank Armenia**: Focus on retail banking (loans, deposits). Offers a **Diaspora Housing Loan** for non-residents.
*   **Mellat Bank**: Iranian bank. Often the only option for Iranians without Armenian citizenship.
*   **VTB Bank Armenia**: Russian subsidiary with a wide branch network.

### Armenian Banks
*   **Acba Bank**: Leading institution, exclusive partner of American Express. Good digital platform.
*   **Ameriabank**: Universal bank with corporate and retail services. Offers premium services.
*   **Araratbank**: Offers various loans and "ArCa Child" cards.
*   **Ardshinbank**: Large network, focuses on project financing and retail. Recently acquired HSBC Armenia.
*   **Artsakhbank**: Assists SMEs, offers pension accounts.
*   **Evocabank**: Positions itself as the first digital bank. Offers "Affordable Housing for Young Families" loans.
*   **Amio Bank**: Offers high deposit savings rates.
*   **Armeconombank**: Offers "Women in business" trade loans.
*   **ArmSwissBank**: Private investment bank offering wealth management services.
*   **Converse Bank**: Good for e-commerce and trade financing. Offers access to Zvartnots business lounge for some cardholders.
*   **Inecobank**: User-friendly app (InecoMobile), InecoPay, and high savings rates. Good for opening accounts for Iranian-Armenians.
*   **Unibank**: Specializes in retail and private banking.
*   **ID Bank**: Offers micro-loans and energy-efficient renovation loans ("Jerm Ojakh").
*   **Fastbank**: 24/7 services in branches.

## ATMs and Payments
*   **ATMs**: Widely available. Dispense **AMD only**. Watch out for withdrawal fees.
*   **Card Payments**: Accepted in most stores (even small ones). Visa and Mastercard are standard.
*   **Credit Cards**: Less common than debit cards; high interest rates.

## Currency Exchange
*   **Apps**: Use `rate.am` to find the best exchange rates.
*   **Locations**: Banks, supermarkets (SAS often has good rates), and exchange kiosks.

## Loans
*   **Guarantors**: Often required. A guarantor must be a legal adult, resident/citizen, with steady income. They take on full responsibility if you default.

## Savings Accounts
*   **Interest Rates**: AMD accounts offer the highest rates (up to ~10%), followed by USD, then EUR.
*   **Tax**: 10% income tax is deducted from interest earned.
